Stopping the Pain, Saving the Tooth

Sudden, severe toothache — especially the kind that wakes you up at night, throbs constantly, or doesn’t settle with painkillers — is often a sign that the nerve is acutely inflamed or infected. A root canal is usually the quickest way to relief.

On the same day, we’ll examine, take a small X-ray, and open the tooth to relieve the pressure. Most patients walk out with the pain already significantly reduced. The full root canal is then completed over one or two follow-up visits.

Far from being something to fear, an emergency root canal is the most dependable way to keep your natural tooth and stop the pain at its source — without resorting to extraction.
