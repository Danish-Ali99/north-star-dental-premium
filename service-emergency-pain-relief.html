Diagnosing the Cause — Stopping the Pain

Tooth pain has many causes: deep decay, a cracked tooth, exposed root, abscess, sinus involvement, or a problem with the bite. The right relief depends entirely on the cause — which is why we never just ‘throw painkillers’ at it.

On the same day, we’ll examine, take a focused X-ray, and identify the specific tooth and the specific problem. Pain relief follows immediately — whether that’s opening an infected tooth, smoothing a sharp edge, replacing a lost filling, or starting antibiotics for swelling.

Definitive treatment (a filling, root canal, crown, or extraction) is then planned calmly, around your life, once you’re comfortable.
