Root Canal Treatment: Your Path to Pain Relief

The phrase ‘root canal’ makes people wince — but the procedure is what ends the pain, clears the infection, and saves your natural tooth.

By Dr. Vartika Kumari · Specialist, Dental Medicine

What exactly is a root canal?

Inside every tooth is a soft core called the pulp — nerves, blood vessels, and connective tissue. When that pulp becomes inflamed or infected (from deep decay, repeated dental work, a crack, or trauma), it can cause severe pain and sensitivity.

Root canal treatment removes the damaged pulp, cleans and disinfects the canal space, and seals it. The tooth is then restored with a filling or, more often, a protective crown.

How do you know if you need one?

Tell-tale signs include:

  • Ongoing pain, or sensitivity to hot and cold that lingers
  • Discomfort when chewing or biting
  • Swelling around the gum or face
  • A darkened or discoloured tooth
  • A small, pus-filled bump near the tooth

When to contact your dentist

See us promptly if you have lingering pain in one tooth, visible damage, swelling, or sensitivity that won’t fade. The earlier you act, the simpler and more successful the treatment tends to be.

Modern root canals are comfortable

With strong local anaesthesia and refined tools, most patients find treatment no more eventful than a routine filling — and many feel immediate relief from the pain they arrived with. Cared for well, a root canal-treated tooth can last for decades.
