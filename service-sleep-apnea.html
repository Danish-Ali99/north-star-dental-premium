A Dental Solution to a Sleep Problem

Obstructive sleep apnea occurs when the tongue and soft tissues collapse into the airway during sleep, partially or completely blocking breathing. The resulting repeated awakenings fragment sleep and deprive the body of restorative rest.

For mild to moderate sleep apnea, a custom mandibular advancement device (MAD) is a proven, effective alternative to CPAP therapy. The appliance holds the lower jaw slightly forward during sleep, keeping the airway open.

We work alongside sleep physicians to ensure appropriate diagnosis and treatment. An oral appliance made by a dentist is far more comfortable and travel-friendly than CPAP equipment, and compliance rates are significantly higher.

Frequently Asked Questions About Sleep Apnea & Snoring Solutions

Dentists can treat mild to moderate obstructive sleep apnea with custom oral appliances. Severe sleep apnea requires medical management, often with CPAP. We work in coordination with sleep physicians.
A CPAP machine uses air pressure delivered through a mask to keep the airway open. An oral appliance is a small, quiet, maskless device you wear in your mouth — more comfortable and portable.
An official diagnosis requires a sleep study conducted by a sleep specialist or physician. If you have not had one, we can advise you on the next step. We do not begin appliance therapy without a diagnosis.
Many patients notice improvement in snoring and sleep quality within the first week of wearing the appliance. It may take a few weeks of adjustment to reach the most effective jaw position.
