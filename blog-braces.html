Braces: A Straighter, Healthier Smile

Dr. Vartika Kumari · 15 May 2026

A healthy smile is more than attractive — it’s a sign of confidence, care, and long-term well-being. Braces are one of the most reliable ways to get there.

Braces are an effective orthodontic treatment for straightening teeth, correcting bite problems, and aligning the jaw. Whether you’re a teenager, a young adult, or a parent making a decision for your child, understanding how braces work makes the choice far less daunting.

What are braces?

Braces are dental appliances that apply gentle, continuous pressure to move teeth into better positions over time. Slowly and predictably, that pressure guides each tooth along the right path until the bite and alignment are where they should be. The pay-off is a straighter smile that’s easier to clean, less prone to decay and gum problems, and better aligned for comfortable chewing and clear speech.

Types of braces

There’s no longer a one-size-fits-all approach. The main options are:

Metal braces

The traditional choice — stainless-steel brackets and wires. They’re durable, cost-effective, and highly efficient, and they remain the best tool for more complex movements. Modern metal braces are smaller and more comfortable than the ones you may remember.

Ceramic braces

These work just like metal braces but use tooth-coloured or clear brackets, so they’re far less visible. A popular middle ground for patients who want discretion without moving to aligners.

Clear aligners (like Invisalign)

A discreet, comfortable way to straighten teeth using a series of clear, removable trays. Each set is worn for a couple of weeks before moving to the next, gradually shifting the teeth. Because they’re nearly invisible and lift out for eating and cleaning, they barely interrupt daily life — no food restrictions, and easy brushing and flossing. Aligners suit many mild-to-moderate cases beautifully, though some complex situations are still better served by fixed braces.

What problems do braces fix?

Beyond simply crowded or crooked teeth, braces correct gaps, overbites, underbites, crossbites, and open bites. Left uncorrected, misalignment isn’t only a cosmetic issue — it can contribute to speech difficulties, jaw strain and headaches, uneven tooth wear, and hard-to-clean areas that invite decay and gum disease.

Why you should consider them

Straightening teeth is an investment in long-term oral health, not just appearance. Well-aligned teeth are easier to keep clean, distribute the forces of chewing more evenly, and tend to last longer. Correcting alignment early — particularly during the teenage years, while the jaw is still developing — often yields faster, more lasting results. That said, adults straighten their teeth successfully every day; it’s rarely too late.

What to expect, and caring for braces

Treatment begins with a consultation and records (photos, scans, sometimes X-rays) so we can plan the exact movements. Once braces or aligners are fitted, you’ll have short, regular reviews to adjust them as your teeth respond. You’ll feel mild pressure for a few days after fitting and each adjustment — that’s the teeth moving, and it settles quickly.

Good hygiene matters more than ever during treatment: brush after meals, clean carefully around brackets and wires (or rinse aligners), and keep up your check-ups. When treatment finishes, a retainer holds your new smile in place. Talk to an orthodontist about the right approach for you — a confident smile lasts a lifetime.
