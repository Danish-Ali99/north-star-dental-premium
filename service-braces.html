Trusted Orthodontic Care for Every Smile

Traditional metal braces use stainless-steel brackets and a thin archwire to move teeth into precise alignment. Today’s brackets are roughly half the size of older designs, with rounded edges that are gentler on lips and cheeks.

For complex crowding, severe rotations, or skeletal bite problems, fixed metal braces remain the most reliable option — and often the most cost-effective. They’re also the strongest tool for growing children and teenagers.

Most cases run 18 to 30 months, with adjustments roughly every 4–6 weeks. We use self-ligating systems that mean fewer visits, less friction, and shorter chair-time per appointment.
